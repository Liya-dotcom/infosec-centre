Cyber Warfare

The Stuxnet Operation

Published: June 15, 2023 15 min read

In 2010, security researchers discovered one of the most sophisticated malware ever created - Stuxnet. This digital weapon was designed to sabotage Iran's nuclear program by causing centrifuges to spin out of control while showing normal readings to operators.

Our investigation reveals how this joint US-Israeli operation marked the first publicly known use of a cyberweapon against industrial infrastructure, setting a dangerous precedent in international relations.

Key Findings:

  • Four zero-day exploits used simultaneously
  • Windows rootkit and PLC malware components
  • Designed to spread via USB drives in air-gapped networks
  • Targeted specific Siemens industrial controllers

Social Engineering

The Twitter Bitcoin Scam

Published: May 22, 2023 12 min read

On July 15, 2020, the world watched as high-profile Twitter accounts including Barack Obama, Elon Musk, and Bill Gates suddenly began tweeting Bitcoin scams. What initially appeared to be a massive platform breach turned out to be a social engineering attack on Twitter employees.

We spoke with security professionals involved in the investigation to uncover how a 17-year-old hacker gained access to Twitter's internal admin tools through a combination of phone phishing and insider knowledge.

Attack Timeline:

  1. Hacker researches Twitter employees on LinkedIn
  2. Creates fake internal Twitter VPN portal
  3. Phishes credentials from targeted employees
  4. Gains access to admin panel through internal VPN
  5. Takes over 130 accounts including verified profiles

Silk Road's Rise and Fall

Published: April 30, 2023 20 min read

The Silk Road marketplace became the most notorious dark web site of its time, generating over $1.2 billion in Bitcoin transactions before its shutdown in 2013. Its creator, Ross Ulbricht (aka Dread Pirate Roberts), built an anonymous marketplace that operated like an eBay for illegal goods.

Our investigative team reviewed court documents and conducted interviews to piece together how the FBI eventually identified and arrested Ulbricht, despite his sophisticated operational security measures.

Key Security Flaws:

  • Early registration using personal email
  • Forum posts linking to clearnet accounts
  • Bitcoin transaction analysis
  • Undercover agent infiltration
